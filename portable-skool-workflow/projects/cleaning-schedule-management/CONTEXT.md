# Context

## What We Are Solving

Cleaning schedules change often. Existing accounts may need to move, and new jobs may need to be added while the current calendar is already full.

The workflow keeps placeholders, conflicts, reminders, and next actions visible.

## Desired Outcome

A good session should produce:

- confirmed jobs
- tentative jobs
- jobs that need to move
- placeholders to resolve
- new jobs to schedule
- recommended schedule options
- reminder actions
- missing information

## What Good Looks Like

- Confirmed and tentative items are separate.
- Placeholder items are not forgotten.
- Conflicts and overloaded days are visible.
- Reminder timing is called out.
- The next action is clear.

## What To Avoid

- Do not bury placeholders in the calendar.
- Do not suggest a slot without noting tradeoffs.
- Do not mix confirmed jobs with tentative options.
- Do not ignore reminder timing.

## Simple Workflow

1. Intake: collect current calendar, new jobs, reschedule requests, and constraints.
2. Organize: separate confirmed jobs, moved jobs, placeholders, open slots, and unscheduled work.
3. Draft: recommend schedule options and reminder actions.
4. Review: check unresolved placeholders, overloads, missing details, and reminders due soon.
